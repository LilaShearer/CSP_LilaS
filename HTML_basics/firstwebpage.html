Poetry: Music in the Written Word

Why should I write poetry?

Poetry allows you to express yourself in a way you cannot otherwise. Emotion flows from your fingertips, decluttering your brain and helping you find peace. Poetry is a personal thing. You don't need to share it with others and it doesn't need to be perfect. It's an escape from the awful perfectionism many experience in their school or work life. You can write a poem that has no meaning to anybody but you! Not only is it a beautiful form of art, but it can improve your writing skills through usage of metaphors, similies, and many other parts of language.

Does poetry need to rhyme?

No, poetry doesn't need to rhyme. Poetry is whatever you want it to be! It can rhyme, it can be free verse, or it can be a bunch of words smushed together that only you know the meaning of. The restrictions that exist in normal writing are totally removed and you are given the space to make decisions. Poetry is freedom.

What do I enjoy about poetry?

I love poetry because I don't need to perform for anybody but myself. I can invent words, rhyme or choose not to, organize my text in a way besides a paragraph, and truly say what I want to. Poetry is music notes ringing across my paper, my pen, my guitar, playing softly and sweetly. I can escape the confines of my anxiety, my fears, the world I live in. It's like dreaming while awake. Poetry has changed my life.

Tips for writing and recommendations for reading

Writing tips

Writing poetry can seem like a daunting task. There are many masters who make it looks so easy. Here are a few tips to help.

  1. Don't overthink it. Poetry doesn't need to be perfect or rhyme in a remarkable, inspiring way.
  2. If rhyming stresses you out, leave behind all boundaries. Write in any form you want.
  3. If you do want to rhyme but cannot think of a rhyme for a particular word, try the website RhymeZone.

Reading recommendations

Robert Frost
Frost's poetry is wonderful. I highly recommend reading his poem 'Spring Pools'. It truly is a work of art. His poetry has very deep meanings that I sometimes miss, but when I notice and understand them, they can be lifechanging.
Katherine Fowler Philips
Philips was a poet during the Renaissance. Her works focus on human relationship and the highs and lows that come with it. Her works are incredible.
Bill Watterson
Yes, I'm serious! Bill Watterson wrote some hilarious poems. I highly recommend reading not only his poetry, but his comics (Calvin and Hobbes) as well.
Shel Silverstein
Silverstein was a master. His works are so funny and have hilarious illustrations. I highly recommend.
